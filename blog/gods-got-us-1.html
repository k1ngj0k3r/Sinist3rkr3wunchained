God’s Got Us — Even When We Don’t See the Plan

Real life. Real pressure. Real faith.

Last night, I was walking back from QT with a gallon of milk for my son.

It wasn’t anything special. Just another late-night walk, making sure we had what we needed at home. But somewhere in that moment, I stopped, looked up at the sky, and asked something real:

“God… what do you have planned for us? I wish you could just tell me straight.”

Not in a spiritual feeling. Not in signs. Just… tell me.

Because right now, life feels like a mix of pressure, responsibility, and trying to make the right decisions for my family.

We’re looking for a place to live. We’ve seen options that looked okay at first, but when we really paid attention, they weren’t right. One place had the electrical panel in my kids’ room. Another had unsafe setups. Another just didn’t feel right.

And then there was a moment where I realized something:

Not every open door is meant for you.

Some doors are there to test if you’re paying attention.

I talked to my pastor about one of the places we saw, and he said something simple:

“That doesn’t sound like the place for you.”

And he was right.

It wasn’t.

It’s easy to feel frustrated when you don’t have clear answers.

When you’re doing everything you can:

Taking care of your kids
Trying to make money
Trying to find stability
Trying to stay strong

And still feel like you’re waiting on something.

But then I started thinking…

Maybe God doesn’t always answer with a voice.

Maybe sometimes the answer looks like:

A bad situation not working out
A deal not going through
A feeling in your gut that says “this ain’t it”

Maybe the direction is already there—we just want it louder.

Today wasn’t perfect.

But we:

Cleaned up the boys’ room
Took a walk as a family
Got groceries
Rested a little

And somehow… that felt like progress.

There’s a verse that says: “Iron sharpens iron.”

And I’ve been listening to a song with that same message.

And it hit me:

Maybe this season isn’t about having everything figured out.

Maybe it’s about being sharpened.

I don’t have all the answers.

I don’t know exactly where we’re going next.

But I do know this:

God’s got us.

And right now, that’s enough to keep moving forward.

If you’re in a place where things feel uncertain…

Just know: You’re not alone in it.

Keep moving. Keep paying attention. Keep trusting.

The plan might not be loud…

But it’s there.

— Ryan
